Victory Park

In the northern part of Tambov lies the largest park in the city, established in 1995 on the site of a wasteland. This park can be considered a memorial gifted to the citizens for the 50th anniversary of the end of the Great Patriotic War. At first, it may seem like 'just another boring Soviet park,' but it is worth visiting to understand what Tambov's Victory Park is all about.

Alley of Heroes

The alley features informational stands with brief historical overviews of all the wars in which Rus, the USSR, or Russia participated. From the Russo-Byzantine War of 907 to the armed conflict in South Ossetia in 2008.

In Victory Park, there is an exhibition of military equipment

The T-55

The T-55 is a Soviet-made main battle tank that was first produced in the early 1950s. It is an evolution of the earlier T-54 tank and features a number of improvements, including a more powerful engine, enhanced armor, and a 100mm rifled gun. The T-55 was designed for mobility, firepower, and protection, making it effective in various combat scenarios. The tank has a low profile and a well-sloped hull, which contributes to its armor effectiveness. It was widely exported and used by many countries around the world, participating in numerous conflicts during the Cold War and beyond. The T-55 remains in service in some nations today, showcasing its durability and adaptability as a combat vehicle.

The T-64

The T-64 is a Soviet main battle tank that was first introduced in the early 1960s. It was one of the first tanks to feature composite armor and a smoothbore gun, specifically a 125mm cannon, which allowed it to fire both conventional rounds and guided missiles. The T-64 was designed to be more compact and lighter than its predecessors, enhancing its mobility and tactical flexibility. Equipped with advanced technology for its time, including an autoloader that reduced the crew size to three, the T-64 offered improved firepower and protection. It was produced in large numbers and saw service in various conflicts, particularly during the Cold War. The tank has undergone numerous upgrades and modifications, and while it has been largely replaced by more modern tanks, it remains in service in several countries.

The BMP-1

The BMP-1 is a Soviet amphibious infantry fighting vehicle that was first introduced in the early 1960s. It was designed to transport infantry into battle while providing fire support. The BMP-1 features a fully tracked chassis, allowing it to traverse various terrains, and it is capable of swimming. Equipped with a 73mm smoothbore gun and a 7.62mm machine gun, the BMP-1 can engage both ground and aerial targets. It also has a troop compartment that can carry up to eight soldiers, providing them with protection from small arms fire and shrapnel. The BMP-1 was one of the first vehicles of its kind and has been widely exported, seeing service in numerous conflicts around the world. Its design has influenced many subsequent infantry fighting vehicles, and it remains in use in various countries today, often undergoing upgrades to enhance its capabilities.

The M46

The M46 is a 130mm towed artillery piece developed by the Soviet Union in the late 1940s. It was designed for long-range bombardment and was notable for its high mobility and firepower. The M46 features a split trail carriage, allowing for quick deployment and repositioning. It has a maximum range of approximately 27 kilometers (about 17 miles) with standard shells and can fire a variety of ammunition types, including high-explosive and chemical rounds. The M46 saw service in various conflicts and was used by several countries, particularly during the Cold War. Its effectiveness and reliability have made it a notable piece of artillery history.

The BRDM

The BRDM is a Soviet amphibious armored reconnaissance vehicle developed in the late 1950s. It is designed for reconnaissance, patrol, and command roles. The BRDM features a lightweight hull, four-wheel drive, and can be equipped with various armaments, including machine guns and anti-tank missiles. It is notable for its amphibious capability, allowing it to operate in water as well as on land. The vehicle has been widely used by various countries and has seen action in numerous conflicts around the world. Its versatility and mobility make it an important asset in reconnaissance operations.

Planar dry fountain

The square where the flat dry fountain is located is equally popular among all age groups. The fountain in Victory Park was installed for the 65th anniversary of the victory in the Great Patriotic War. It is a light and music fountain, and in the evening, visitors can witness a real show as its jets soar up to 12 meters high to the music. During the day, the fountain operates in a children's mode, with jets that do not rise higher than one and a half meters, allowing children to play safely here.

The park also features a "children's area" - here you will find a free playground and the "Victory" amusement park, which has figures of various characters from cartoons.

In the depths of the park, there is a network of walking paths and alleys. Notably, in the "farthest corner" of the park, there are isolated groups of trees of various species: aspen, linden, spruce, birch, and white acacia.
